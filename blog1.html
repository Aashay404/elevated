How Play-Based Learning Builds Strong Foundations

February 12, 2025 Dr. Sarah Chen Child Development

In the rush to prepare children for academic success, unstructured play is often undervalued. Yet research consistently shows that play is the primary way young children learn about their world, develop social skills, and build cognitive flexibility. At Elevated Minds, we integrate play-based learning across our early childhood programs.

Why play matters

Play is not just about having fun—it's a complex learning process. When children build with blocks, they explore physics and balance. When they engage in pretend play, they develop narrative skills and empathy. Through play, children learn to negotiate, solve problems, and regulate emotions.

Structured vs. unstructured play

Both have value. Structured play (like guided games) teaches rules and cooperation. Unstructured play allows children to follow their curiosity, make discoveries, and develop creativity. A balanced approach is key.

Our educators observe children during play to understand their interests and developmental needs, then gently extend their learning with thoughtful questions and materials.
