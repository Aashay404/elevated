One Woman Every 2 Minutes — Act Now

Every year, about 660,000 women are diagnosed with cervical cancer worldwide and around 350,000 die from it,roughly one woman every 2 minutes. Most of these deaths occur in low- and middle-income countries, and nearly all cases are caused by persistent HPV infection. The good news? Cervical cancer can be prevented with HPV vaccination and regular screening, yet many women miss these lifesaving steps.

SHE SURVIVED!

“My journey wasn’t easy, but the support from my doctor and family kept me going. After surgery and therapy, my reports are clear — I’m healthy and grateful. It was a battle of mind and body, but I never gave up. CCPF’s awareness program helped me understand that cancer caught early can be cured”

“At 34, Meena went for a routine health camp organized by CCPF. She felt perfectly healthy but decided to get a Pap smear anyway. The test revealed early-stage changes in her cervix. Quick treatment and follow-ups later, she was cancer-free.
Getting tested early didn’t just save my life — it gave me years to watch my children grow.”

“Latha had never heard of the HPV vaccine until her best friend got hers through CCPF. Encouraged, she got vaccinated too. Two years later, a screening detected early changes — caught just in time. Now she inspires her entire community to get vaccinated
