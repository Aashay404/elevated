What is CCPF?

CCPF stands for Cervical Cancer Prevention Foundation. It is a health awareness initiative dedicated to preventing cervical cancer through early screening, vaccination, and public education.

What is the HPV vaccine and why is it important?

The HPV vaccine protects against the human papillomavirus, which causes most cervical cancer cases. HPV infection is very common and often shows no symptoms. The vaccine helps stop the infection before it can cause cell changes. It greatly lowers the risk of cervical cancer and other HPV-related diseases.

Who should get the HPV vaccine?

The vaccine is recommended for girls and boys aged 9–14 years. It can also be taken by adults up to 45 years after consulting a doctor. Vaccination works best when given before exposure to HPV. Even adults benefit from added protection.

Is the HPV vaccine safe?

Yes, the HPV vaccine is safe and approved worldwide. It has been used for many years in many countries. Most side effects are mild, such as pain or swelling at the injection site. Serious side effects are very rare.

What is a Pap smear test?

A Pap smear is a simple screening test for cervical health. It checks for abnormal or pre-cancerous cell changes. The test is quick and usually takes only a few minutes. Early detection helps prevent cervical cancer.

Why is the Pap smear test necessary?

Cervical cancer often develops without early symptoms. Pap smear tests detect problems before cancer forms. Early treatment can stop cancer from developing. Regular screening saves lives.

Is the Pap smear test painful?

The test is usually not painful. Some women may feel mild pressure or discomfort. The procedure is quick and safe. Any discomfort lasts only a short time.

What are the early signs of cervical cancer?

Early stages often have no noticeable symptoms. Many women feel completely healthy. Symptoms appear only when the disease progresses. This is why regular screening is very important.
